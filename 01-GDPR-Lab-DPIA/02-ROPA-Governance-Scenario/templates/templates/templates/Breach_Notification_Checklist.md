# Breach Notification Checklist (Draft)

This checklist provides a structured workflow for determining whether a security incident is notifiable under GDPR and outlines the steps required for proper documentation and communication.

---

## 1. Confirm Incident Occurrence
- Has an event occurred that affects personal data?
- Is there unauthorized access, disclosure, loss, alteration, or destruction?

---

## 2. Identify Personal Data Involved
- Categories of personal data affected
- Number of individuals involved
- Sensitivity level (e.g., identifiers, financial, health, children's data)

---

## 3. Assess Likelihood and Severity of Risk
Evaluate potential impact on individualsâ€™:
- Confidentiality  
- Integrity  
- Availability  
- Rights and freedoms (financial loss, identity theft, emotional harm)

---

## 4. Determine Notifiability
### Notify the Supervisory Authority if:
- There is a risk to individuals  
- Notification required **within 72 hours** of becoming aware  

### Notify Data Subjects if:
- There is a high risk to rights and freedoms  
- Notification must be done **without undue delay**

---

## 5. Required Elements of Notification
### Notification to Supervisory Authority
Include:
- Nature of the breach  
- Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected  
- Categories and approximate number of data records involved  
- Contact details of the DPO or responsible person  
- Likely consequences  
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach  

### Notification to Data Subjects
Include:
- Clear description of the breach  
- Likely consequences  
- Guidance on steps they can take to protect themselves  
- Measures taken to mitigate effects  
- Contact information  

---

## 6. Document the Incident Internally
Create an entry in the incident log including:
- Date/time detected  
- Incident description  
- System(s) affected  
- Root cause  
- Actions taken  
- Legal/regulatory decisions  
- Final resolution and closure date  

Document **every breach**, even if it is not notifiable.

---

## 7. Follow-Up Actions
- Review effectiveness of security measures  
- Update controls or training  
- Reassess risk posture  
- Add lessons learned to improve incident response

---

## 8. Last Review Date
Include:
- Reviewer name  
- Date of last update
